In the 1950s John Cage wrote a series of random musical pieces using the 64 hexagrams from the I Ching. The hexagrams could be constructed in different ways. Cage tossed 6 times 3 coins to construct each hexagram. Each hexagram defined some parameter of the piece (number of voices, height, duration value, etc). The computer simulates the flips and draws a hexagram.

There are 64

As hexagrams are groups of 6 lines, which can be cut or not, there are 64 different ones. When I ask the computer to draw 64 at random it does not find all of them. Cage used 64-cell tables to link each hexagram to a decision.
